Executive Summary
2026 Recruitment Library · Corrected Edition
Thirty-three active protocols span four IPMG sites — Chino (18), Redlands (3), Temecula (7), and San Juan
Capistrano (5) — forming the 2026 recruitment library.
Coverage includes Major Depressive Disorder (monotherapy, adjunct, TRD, and biomarker-matched),
Bipolar I and II depression and mania, Schizophrenia (adult and adolescent), Alzheimer's psychosis and
agitation, Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder, Generalized Anxiety Disorder, Binge Eating Disorder, Tardive
Dyskinesia, pediatric bipolar, pediatric schizophrenia, autism irritability, and psychedelic / neuroplastogen
programs (psilocybin, methylone).
Each study brief follows an 18-section format: Identification, Hook, Unmet Need, At-a-Glance, Why This
Study Exists, Mechanism of Action, Study Design Deep Dive, Recruit If, Stop/Exclude, Ideal Patient Profile,
eCW + ICD-10 search plan, Competitive Landscape, Risk/Benefit, Why This Matters for a Large Psychiatric
Group, Provider Talk Track, 3-Step Recruit Move, Final CTA, and Sources.
Screening for every protocol routes through IPMG Research at (909) 955-5865.
This library is a working reference. Each brief ends with a specific comment code and a one-week screening
ask. Save it, screen one patient per study per week, and the aggregate enrollment contribution across 33
protocols becomes category-defining for Southern California psychiatric research.
n 2026 CORRECTION

What Changed in v2.0
Eight factual corrections from v1.0 of the 2026 library
STUDY 02 · NORLIGHT (ACP-211 in MDD)
Corrected to TRD adjunct design. Patients must currently be on an approved antidepressant AND have
documented history of ≥2 prior antidepressant failures in the current episode. AD-naive and washed-out
patients are NOT eligible. Recruiting the right patients requires searching FOR refractory cases — not
screening them out.
STUDY 03 · ALTOBIP (ALTO-100 in Bipolar Depression)
Corrected to 6-week double-blind period (not 8 weeks) with primary MADRS endpoint at Week 6, followed
by an open-label period of up to 7 weeks.
STUDY 04 · CLARITY (Solriamfetol MDD + EDS)
Corrected to randomized-withdrawal design with open-label lead-in. All eligible participants FIRST
receive open-label solriamfetol. Only responders are then randomized 1:1 to continue drug or withdraw to
placebo. Every patient is guaranteed active drug at the start — a material pitch point.
STUDY 08 · BALSAM (KarXT BALSAM-4 in Bipolar I Mania)
Corrected to strictly adjunctive design. Patients MUST currently be on — and remain on throughout the
trial — lithium, valproate, or lamotrigine. KarXT or placebo is added on top. Do NOT screen patients
looking to switch off their mood stabilizer.
STUDY 14 · M4OLE (NBI-1117568 Schizophrenia OLE)
Corrected sponsor attribution: emraclidine is an AbbVie asset (acquired via the $8.7B Cerevel deal), NOT
a Bristol Myers Squibb asset. BMS owns the competitor, KarXT (Cobenfy). Emraclidine failed its Phase 2
schizophrenia trials in November 2024.
STUDY 24 · KORMDD (Icalcaprant MDD)
Corrected target enrollment to ~195 (not ~500). Corrected exclusion criterion: patients are excluded only
for ≥3 prior antidepressant failures. Patients with exactly 2 prior failures ARE eligible — the v1.0 brief
would have screened them out incorrectly.

CHINO · STUDY 01 · COMMENT CODE YOUTHLIFT
Cariprazine (Vraylar) in Pediatric Bipolar I
Depression
PROTOCOL
3112-301-001
NCT
NCT04777357
SPONSOR
AbbVie / Allergan
SITE
Chino
COMMENT CODE
YOUTHLIFT
1 · THE HOOK
STILL watching a 13-year-old sink into bipolar depression while the FDA only
approves three drugs for this age group?
2 · THE UNMET NEED
Pediatric bipolar depression affects ~1% of youth and drives the highest suicide risk in child psychiatry. Only
lurasidone, olanzapine-fluoxetine, and lumateperone carry pediatric bipolar-depression labels. Parents and
clinicians are left choosing between metabolic risk and weight gain.
H A D2/D3 partial agonist with a clean metabolic profile could reset the pediatric
bipolar-depression standard of care.
3 · AT A GLANCE
10-17 6 wks 1.5-3 mg ~380
AGE RANGE DOUBLE-BLIND FLEX DOSE TARGET N
4 · WHY THIS STUDY EXISTS
Cariprazine is already approved for adult bipolar I depression, mania, schizophrenia, and MDD adjunct. This
Phase 3 bridges to pediatric label — a gap the field has begged to fill.
5 · MECHANISM OF ACTION
D3-preferring D2/D3 partial agonist + 5-HT1A partial agonist + 5-HT2B antagonist. Long half-life (1-3 weeks
for active metabolite) provides forgiving dosing in adolescents prone to missing doses.
6 · STUDY DESIGN DEEP DIVE
Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, flexible-dose 6-week monotherapy study. Primary endpoint:
change in CDRS-R total. Key secondary: CGI-BP-S depression. Includes 26-week open-label extension.
7 · RECRUIT IF YOUR PATIENT…
Your patient is 10-17, has a confirmed DSM-5 Bipolar I diagnosis, is currently in a major depressive episode
(CDRS-R >=45), and hasn't responded to or tolerated first-line options. The whole point: catch bipolar
depression before suicide attempts and school failure compound.
8 · STOP / EXCLUDE IF…
7 Active suicidal plan/intent (C-SSRS item 4-5 past month)
7 Substance use disorder past 6 months (except nicotine)
